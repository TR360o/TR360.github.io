The GlucoGuard

Let's prevent Diabetes Type II together!

The GlucoGuard with slogan.

Diabetes type 2 has become a pressing concern in our society, emerging as one of the most prevalent health issues. Particularly affecting individuals with lower socioeconomic status (SES) in our society.The severity of this condition goes beyond individual health, significantly straining healthcare systems and impacting overall societal well-being. Unhealthy lifestyle choices, limited access to healthy options, and economic hardships contribute to the alarming rise in diabetes type 2 cases. Urgent efforts are required to address this epidemic, promoting healthier living and financial feasibility for individuals to adopt healthier lifestyles. Yet, initiating this positive change starts with a minor adjustment, and that's where the GlucoGuard comes into play.

For this project, I have engaged in a collaboration with Deloitte and LIME (Limburg Meet), aiming to find a solution for the escalating pressure in healthcare through an innovative concept. LIME is assisting me in the technical aspects of the project by involving students from the medical field to provide insights and conduct health tests. During a test day in Roermond, I was allowed to interview pedestrians to gather feedback on the project.

The objective was initially broad, prompting my decision to refine it. It was swiftly determined that the project's focus would center on type 2 diabetes. Statistics indicate that 1 in 3 Dutch individuals aged 45 and above will develop type 2 diabetes in their lifetime, leading to associated conditions like cardiovascular diseases. Moreover, many individuals in my immediate circle are living with this condition.

Once individuals progress beyond the pre-diabetes stage, it becomes irreversible. Consequently, my goal is to "heighten awareness about the pre-diabetes phase and provide effective support."

Current technology still faces challenges in non-invasively measuring blood glucose. Initially, I proposed a self-pricking station in supermarkets, allowing customers to perform a finger prick. However, after testing, it became evident that customers were reluctant due to needle aversion, hygienic, and privacy concerns.

After extensive research, I discovered the German company DiaMonTech, actively developing the D-Pocket. This small device measures blood glucose levels using light, eliminating the need for needles. It is anticipated that this product will be available in the market within a few years.

Building upon this technology, I conceptualized GlucoGuard—an app extension functioning as an overlay on existing applications like the AH app. GlucoGuard will also integrate with other supermarket-connected apps. For this project, we assume the utilization of the AH app.

The GlucoGuard is a companion that assists you in making informed decisions at the supermarket regarding sugar intake. It alerts you with a bark when you scan a product high in sugar content and recommends a substitute with lower sugar, if needed. If a product is already low in sugars, a green label will be displayed next to it. The app also features recipes marked with this label. On your profile, you can track how much sugar and money you've saved with each purchase.

Beyond offering advice, GlucoGuard helps you take measurements in the supermarket. This process occurs in a small booth near a screened pole at the information desk. A service worker can assist you with a simple button press if the measurement proves challenging. To take the measurement, scan your loyalty card and follow the steps on the screen. Later, you can view or print your results at the pole through the application.

The prototype of the GlucoGuard overlay extension is available in the Figma file below. It's designed at a high-fidelity level and adopts the visual identity of the AH (Albert Heijn) application. If used in other supermarket applications, the GlucoGuard extension adapts to their respective visual identities while maintaining the same functionality.
